Species of the Day
Tree of the Day
Banyan Tree
Ficus benghalensis

India's national tree, the Banyan is a strangler fig that begins life as a seed in another tree's canopy. Its iconic aerial roots descend to the ground, forming new trunks that allow a single tree to spread over several hectares. The Great Banyan in Kolkata covers over 3.5 acres — making it the widest tree in the world.

Interesting Facts

What makes them extraordinary

🌳
Peepal · Ficus religiosa
The only tree that exhales oxygen 24 hours a day

Unlike most plants that release CO₂ at night, the sacred Peepal performs Crassulacean Acid Metabolism (CAM), enabling continuous oxygen production.

🐯
Bengal Tiger · Panthera tigris tigris
Each tiger's stripes are as unique as a human fingerprint

No two tigers share the same stripe pattern. Wildlife conservationists use stripe photography to track individuals through dense forests without disturbing them.

🎋
Bamboo · Bambusoideae
A grass that outgrows any tree — up to 91 cm in one day

Technically a grass, certain bamboo species recorded in India's Northeast grow up to 91 cm in 24 hours, making it the world's fastest growing plant.

🐘
Asian Elephant · Elephas maximus
Elephants communicate through seismic vibrations in the ground

Asian elephants produce low-frequency rumbles — some inaudible to humans — that travel through the earth as seismic waves detectable from 30 kilometres away.

🌴
Areca Palm · Areca catechu
A palm that sustains 600 million people's daily ritual

The areca nut (supari) is chewed daily by an estimated 600 million people globally — making it the fourth most widely consumed psychoactive substance.

🦜
Indian Peacock · Pavo cristatus
The train isn't a tail — and it's controlled by UV vision

The peacock's spectacular "tail" is actually its upper tail covert feathers. Peahens choose mates partly based on UV reflectance patterns invisible to human eyes.
